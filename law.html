IMPERIAL CODEX

LAW AND ORDER

Discipline · Judgment · Control

The Empire classifies every breach by severity. Review the tribunal classes, open each charge, and understand the standard that keeps Sith order absolute.

Class A Offenses · 5
A-1
Misconduct
›
Misconduct is defined as consistent immature conduct, trolling, or disruptive behavior that negatively impacts the community or interferes with official activities. Includes being a nuisance during trainings, tryouts, combat duels, or other events. Intentionally provoking, bothering, intimidating, taunting, or irritating another individual or group. Disrupting conversations, activities, or events through repeated or unnecessary actions. Does not include walking over, re-banging, or t-bagging hostile individuals after death, or consensual banter between willing participants.
A-2
Abuse of Force
›
Abuse of Force is defined as forcibly moving, pushing, or otherwise interfering with another individual without their consent. Includes shoving, pushing, or any form of forced movement that disrupts activities or compromises safety. Using Force abilities or saber throw against allies without permission. Intentionally moving individuals into dangerous, restricted, or combat-related areas.
A-3
Insubordination
›
Insubordination is defined as the refusal, disregard, or failure to comply with lawful orders issued by a superior, whether intentional or unintentional. "Superior" refers to an individual recognized by the official hierarchy. Refusing to carry out lawful orders or deliberately ignoring instructions during operations, trainings, defenses, or organized events also constitutes insubordination.
A-4
Inadequate Attire
›
Inadequate Attire is defined as participating in events or activities without the appropriate uniform, or wearing attire deemed inappropriate for The Sith Empire. → Includes attire representing factions other than The Sith Empire. → Includes unrealistic, obtrusive, particle-inducing, flashy, or excessively bright accessories. → Includes attire intended to mimic or impersonate Dark Honor Guard personnel. → Includes outfits that do not contain the required Classic Clothing Shirt and Pants. → Includes wearing unapproved clothing during official events, trainings, inspections, or operations. Examples • Wearing another faction’s uniform or insignia during TSE activities. • Using oversized particle accessories or excessively bright cosmetic items. • Impersonating Dark Honor Guard personnel through attire. • Attending an official event without the required base uniform components.
A-5
Improper Combat
›
Improper Combat is defined as engaging in combat or dueling outside of authorized duel conditions. Includes refusing to properly duel while in a duel in order to gain access to restricted areas, or dueling inside restricted areas. Does NOT include combat occurring within designated combat areas.
Class B Offenses · 6
B-1
Homicide
›
Homicide is defined as the killing of members of The Sith Empire. Includes accidentally killing an individual while engaged in combat with a hostile, or accidentally killing a nearby Sith during an execution performed by the Dark Honor Guard. If the offender is intentionally using Sith members as cover, this offense does not apply.
B-2
Collusion
›
Collusion is defined as the intentional act of assisting, supporting, encouraging, or overlooking the operations of hostiles within the Sith Temple. Includes intentionally helping a hostile enter the Sith Temple, attacking fellow Sith to protect a hostile individual or group, or assisting hostiles in planning attempts to infiltrate or attack the Sith Temple. Does NOT include a Sith member being unable to defeat a hostile due to lack of skill or stamina, or ordinary conversation with hostiles outside the vicinity of the Sith Temple.
B-3
Abandonment
›
Abandonment is defined as the failure of an individual to fulfill their responsibilities in a timely manner without an active Inactivity Notice, or the intentional departure from a Pathway or Organization without proper discharge procedures. Includes but is not limited to specialization leadership duties, trainings and events, and enforcement of law. Does NOT include removal from a Specialization through disciplinary action.
B-4
Admin Abuse
›
Admin Abuse is defined as the misuse of administrative commands without valid reason or authorization. Includes using admin commands to create disorder or unrest, misusing administrative commands to cause severe damage to game assets or players, or using admin commands to maliciously attack players or degrade the gameplay experience.
B-5
Deception
›
Deception is defined as the intentional and severe act of falsifying verbal or online communication, committing perjury by falsely denying proven misconduct, spreading false statements to damage another person's reputation, or impersonating members of the Sith Empire in order to deceive, defraud, or cause significant harm. Includes filing false reports, fabricating or altering evidence, or impersonating members for malicious purposes.
B-6
Trespassing
›
Trespassing is defined as entering restricted areas without permission or authorization. Includes entering the Dark Lord's Throne Room without approval, or entering the Jail Room while Inquisitors are present without authorization.
Class C Offenses · 6
C-1
Endangerment
›
Endangerment is defined as engaging in predatory, inappropriate, or disturbing behavior that negatively impacts the safety and well-being of community members. Includes predatory behavior or harmful user engagement, behavior inappropriate for younger audiences, and disturbing behavior or content unsuitable for community interaction.
C-2
Major Homicide
›
Major Homicide is defined as the mass murder of Sith Empire personnel ranked below the Dark Council. Includes killing multiple individuals within a short period of time, or mass killings intended to troll, harass, or disturb others. If a member of the Dark Council or higher is murdered, the offense is classified as Treason.
C-3
Disloyalty
›
Disloyalty is defined as membership in enemy factions. Includes Karma Raiders.
C-4
Distribution of Classified Information
›
Distribution of Classified Information is defined as the intentional disclosure of confidential or classified information to unauthorized individuals without proper permission. Includes releasing documents intended to remain classified to the public, infiltration or the use of "spies" to obtain restricted information or cause damage, and leaking sensitive information to unauthorized personnel.
C-5
Unlawful Influence
›
Unlawful Influence is defined as manipulating, coercing, or deceiving a Sith member into violating laws or committing offenses within the community. Includes any form of manipulation that results in the violation of community rules or protocols. Example: A Sith Inquisitor convinces an Acolyte to approach a Darth and attempt to kill them. Example: A Sith instructs another Sith to attack or disrupt a class or event.
C-6
Treason
›
Treason is defined as conspiring against, attempting to assassinate, or murdering members of the Dark Council. Includes the intentional or unintentional murder of the Dark Lord or members of the Dark Council, and attempting to kill members of the Dark Council without consent. Example: Killing or attempting to kill a Dark Council member or higher in-game without authorization.
