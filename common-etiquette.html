COMMON ETIQUETTE

Form  ·  Protocol  ·  Precision

Imperial discipline is reflected not only in action but in formation. These standardized formations ensure unity, command clarity, and visual cohesion during all organized imperial activities.

Formation Protocols

Noncombatative Formations

Noncombatative formations are employed during ceremonies, trainings, tryouts, and organized events. Each formation serves a distinct purpose and requires precise positioning to maintain the dignity and order of the Empire.

Standard Formation

Wedge

One of the most commonly used formations. This formation requires 2 or more members arranged in a V-shape, with the leader or superior positioned at the front point of the formation.

  • Requires a minimum of 2 members arranged in a V-shape.
  • The leader or superior is positioned at the front point of the formation.
  • All members should be facing forward, matching the direction the superior is facing.
  • If there is an uneven number of members, the extra member(s) should stand directly behind the superior, centered within the formation.
Training Formation

Side to Side

Frequently used during trainings, tryouts, and organized events. In this formation, members line up horizontally in a straight line.

  • Members line up horizontally in a straight line.
  • Members should face forward towards the superior, or toward a potential threat if the situation requires it.
Standard Formation

Single File Line

Members line up vertically, one behind another, forming a straight line.

  • Members line up vertically, one behind another, forming a straight line.
  • All members should be facing forward, aligned with the direction the superior is facing.
