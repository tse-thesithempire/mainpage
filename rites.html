I.
Chapter One

Tenets of Sith Philosophy

The Dark Lord's Ruminations are a highlight of the Sith Philosophy interpreted by the Dark Lord himself. They represent a multitude of statements that correlate to personal acknowledgement and success. The Ruminations represent responsibility for the responsible, vital existence instead of wallowing bliss, kindness to those who deserve it, undefiled wisdom instead of hypocritical self-deceit, and vengeance.

Stratification
There is no more myth of equality.

It is a fantasy that only translates to mediocrity and supports the weak at the expense of the strong. Individuals must be allowed to seek their own level without interference from apologists for incompetence.

No one should be protected from the consequences of their own actions.
Veracity
There is no more illusion of pretense.

This is a veil that obscures authenticity, elevating superficiality over true merit. The pursuit of status and appearance only serves to undermine the value of sincerity, turning individuals into mere actors playing a part rather than living with purpose.

No one should live in the imitation of greatness.
Insight
There is no more fantasy of subjectivity.

Do not confine the mind to a self-centered reality and ignore the existence and value of others. Understanding emerges when we acknowledge perspectives beyond our own.

No one should believe they are the sole creators of truth.
Reality
There is no more fallacy of delusion.

Delusion is a misconception that clouds judgment, allowing people to cling to comforting lies rather than face uncomfortable truths. It thrives in the mind's refusal to accept personal flaws, preventing growth and understanding.

No one should lack the ability to be honest with themselves and seek clarity.
Autonomy
There is no more fabrication of conformity.

What's right and wrong to do is a construction that suppresses individuality, encouraging compliance with the crowd instead of independent thought. Freedom lies in mastering who you follow, not submitting to the crowd.

No one should ever forget what a threat they can be.
Modesty
There is no more essence of hubris.

Hubris is an ignorance that becomes counterproductive when it blinds you to the reality of a situation. When stubbornness no longer serves you, humility becomes the key to resolution.

No one should ever refuse or lack the strength to acknowledge their mistakes.
II.
Chapter Two

Sith Traditions

Tradition of the Crimson Blade

Bleeding a Kyber crystal is a sacred tradition in the Sith. Unlike the Jedi, we do not attune ourselves with a Kyber crystal. Instead, we impose our will onto the Kyber, which will either break or bend to our will. If the Kyber accepts your will, you will have officially bled your crystal. This is a great milestone, for previous generations of the Sith and many more to come.

Throughout history, few lords of the Sith did not use the traditional color of red in their lightsabers, typically because they only saw lightsabers as mere tools to pity the Jedi for their rudimentary usage of them. The Dark Lord of the Sith Exar Kun was completely oblivious and unaware of the process of bleeding a crystal.

Tradition of the Black Legion

Dressing in dark garments was not a common tradition during the establishment of The Sith Empire. Instead, during the time of the first Sith Empire, the founding Exiles lorded over the Sith with a sense of power and luxury. They wore elaborate robes and armor, decorated with jewels and symbols to show their authority. Their clothes were meant to reflect their high status, not just their role as fighters. The lords of this ancient Sith Empire made sure their attire matched their power and control over the Esstran sector.

As the Sith Empire grew, traditions started to change. The Dark Lords realized that true power wasn't just about strength or status, it was also about fear and intimidation. This led to the creation of the iconic dark robes that the Sith are now known for. These robes became a symbol of the Sith's connection to the Dark Side, representing their rejection of the Light Side.

Tradition of the Solitary Saber

To the Sith, the lightsaber was not something to be wielded out of a sense of humility or defense. It was an instrument of power. While the single blade was revered, the Sith did not dismiss other lightsaber styles outright. The double-bladed lightsaber and the more practical dual-wielding techniques were respected for their own utility. Sith Lords like Exar Kun, Darth Maul, and many others demonstrated that these unorthodox forms of lightsaber combat were effective enhancements of the Sith's command over the Force. The versatility of a double-bladed lightsaber or the fluidity of dual blades spoke to a different kind of mastery; a dynamic, overwhelming style that could devastate an opponent in a flurry of motion.

Many Sith decided against this folly. The blade itself was not special; it was simply an addition to the prowess that flowed through a Sith. A skilled Sith could defeat an entire battalion of Jedi, not by virtue of their weapon, but by virtue of their mastery.

Tradition of the Qotsisajak

The Sith Code emphasizes the importance of ambition and refusing to come to terms with what you believe isn't right. It acknowledges that by using your subconscious emotions such as fear, anger, and hate, you can create the tools necessary to establish the personal foundation and stepping stone for your overall goals as a Sith. It even explicitly outlines twice that fear does not exist and you will never know it, because in order to truly understand The Sith Code you must realize that your second thoughts and self doubts do not exist while you wield the Dark Side of the Force with full confidence.
